Activities for Charlotte

👂
Listening
Sound Detective

Charlotte listens for the first sound in a word.

Steps

  1. 1Adult says one word: sun, ant, tap, pin, insect, or net.
  2. 2Charlotte repeats the word.
  3. 3Charlotte says the first sound only.
  4. 4Charlotte taps the matching letter card.
👆
Letters
Tap My Sound

Charlotte taps a big letter and copies the sound.

Steps

  1. 1Adult points to one SATPIN letter.
  2. 2Charlotte taps it on the screen.
  3. 3Charlotte says the sound three times.
  4. 4Charlotte finds one object with that sound.
🧱
Coding
Push the Sounds

Charlotte pushes counters for each sound in a word.

Steps

  1. 1Pick a word: sat, pin, tap, nap, tin, or pan.
  2. 2Say the whole word.
  3. 3Push one counter for each sound.
  4. 4Say the sounds, then blend the word.
🖍️
Writing
Rainbow Letters

Charlotte traces one letter using different colours.

Steps

  1. 1Choose one sound: s, a, t, p, i, or n.
  2. 2Say the sound while tracing the letter.
  3. 3Trace it with three colours.
  4. 4Draw one picture that starts with that sound.
🏠
Home hunt
Find the Sound

Charlotte finds things around the room with a first sound.

Steps

  1. 1Adult picks one sound.
  2. 2Charlotte looks around the room.
  3. 3Charlotte finds or points to an object.
  4. 4Adult says: yes, it starts with that sound.
🎤
Blending
Robot Talk

Charlotte listens to robot sounds and blends the word.

Steps

  1. 1Adult says sounds slowly, like s-a-t.
  2. 2Charlotte repeats the sounds.
  3. 3Charlotte blends the sounds into a word.
  4. 4Try pin, tin, tap, pan, sip, nap, and pit.

Adult Notes

This site is made for a Year 1 student who is not yet reading. Keep each task short. Use the read-aloud buttons, then ask Charlotte to listen, tap, say, trace, and match.
  • Focus sounds: s, a, t, p, i, n.
  • Use pure sounds where possible. Say /s/ not "suh".
  • Start with listening and matching before asking Charlotte to read words.
  • For coding, use counters or fingers to show one sound per letter.
  • Suggested words: sat, pin, tap, tin, pan, nap, sip, pit, tip.
  • Stop after 10 to 15 minutes if Charlotte becomes tired.
  • Celebrate sound recognition before spelling accuracy.
